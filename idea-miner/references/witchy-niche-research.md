# Witchy/Spiritual Niche Research (May 2026)

## Competitor Landscape

### Subscription Apps (Pain Source)
| App | Pricing | Complaints |
|-----|---------|------------|
| Moonly | $4-40/mo + $15 birth chart upsell | Aggressive paywall, billing issues after updates, 53% negative reviews on JustUseApp |
| Spells8 | $29/mo ($10/mo annual w/ discount) | "Way too expensive" — Reddit consensus: free resources sufficient for experienced practitioners |
| The Pattern | Subscription | Psychological astrology, considered premium |
| CHANI/Nebula | Subscription | Preferred over Moonly for timeline insights |

### Free Alternatives (Competitive Pressure)
- MoonX: moon phase tracking + notes (free/freemium)
- Medito: guided meditation (100% free)
- Insight Timer: meditation library (free)
- Witchipedia wikidot: correspondence tables (free, static)

### Digital Products (Validation Signal)
- Etsy: printable correspondence charts $3-10 (static PDFs, non-searchable)
- Gumroad: Notion grimoire templates $7-15 (static templates, not interactive tools) — **NOTE: Gumroad blocks web_extract**
- GoodNotes/Notability: iPad handwriting apps ($$ one-time)

## Key Pain Points Confirmed

### 1. Subscription Fatigue for Static Data
Correspondences (herbs→planets→colors→crystals) don't change. Users resent paying monthly for reference data. Reddit consensus: "so many sources of spells which don't require a subscription that $10/mo seems like a rip off."

### 2. Digital Grimoire Fragmentation
Witches use Notion/Evernote/GoodNotes but no purpose-built tool. Spells8 forum warning: "be careful with smaller apps... risk losing all your work if app doesn't get updated." Gumroad templates are static — not interactive tools.

### 3. Spell Effectiveness Tracking Gap
No tool tracks what spells actually worked over time. Community uses physical notebooks + digital backups with zero analytics. Reddit discussion on "Planners, Books of Shadows, Grimoires" shows manual tracking only.

## Buyer Profile
- Women 30-65, beginner to intermediate practitioners
- Active: r/witchcraft (450k+), r/Wicca (150k+), Pinterest, TikTok witchy community
- Already spending on crystals, candles, herbs, digital planners ($29/mo subscription boxes)
- Subscription-fatigued but willing to pay one-time for interactive tools

## Mining Tactics That Worked
1. Search `[niche] app review` → found competitor pricing/complaints
2. `site:reddit.com [app name] worth the price` → direct user feedback
3. App review aggregator sites (JustUseApp, Trustpilot) → billing complaints + pricing tiers
4. Etsy/Gumroad category search → validates market demand without needing complaint quotes
5. Niche forums (Spells8 forum) → deep pain point extraction

## Tactics That Failed
- Exact phrase searches `"I wish there was"` on Reddit → consistently empty via web_search
- Gumroad product page scraping → Internal Server Error, blocked by site
- Broad "witchcraft tool" searches → produced affiliate listicles, not pain points
