# Supply Chain Intelligence Analysis

## Use When
- Analyzing competitor or target company supply chains
- Finding white-space business opportunities in component markets
- Assessing single-source vulnerabilities
- Mapping raw material flows and geographic concentration risks

## Steps

### 1. Company Overview & Financials
- Identify parent company (e.g., KitchenAid → Whirlpool Corp, NYSE: WHR)
- Extract segment revenue and margins from earnings reports/10-K
- Note manufacturing footprint (plants, locations)

### 2. Supplier Identification
- Search "[Company] supplier award winners" or "approved vendor list"
- Review 10-K for disclosed suppliers and concentration risks
- Document what each supplier provides + strategic importance level

### 3. Raw Material Flow Mapping
- Identify key inputs (steel, aluminum, resins, copper, etc.)
- Note sourcing geography (% domestic vs import)
- Flag commodity price exposure

### 4. Risk Analysis
**Single-Source Supplier Risk:** Check 10-K for "single-source" disclosures  
**Geographic Concentration:** Map manufacturing locations; flag single-plant dependencies  
**Customer Concentration:** Identify top customers (often in 10-K as % of receivables)  
**Trade/Tariff Exposure:** Note Mexico/China facilities and tariff insulation  
**Raw Material Volatility:** Commodity price sensitivity

### 5. Market Entry Opportunities
Generate white-space plays based on identified vulnerabilities:
- Component diversification (alternative supplier networks)
- Near-shore manufacturing (tariff-advantaged locations)
- Supply chain transparency/certification services
- Aftermarket parts ecosystems
- Material innovation (bio-based, recycled composites)

## Verification Checklist
- [ ] Company overview and industry identified
- [ ] 10+ suppliers documented
- [ ] Top 5 suppliers deep-dived with strategic importance
- [ ] Raw materials mapped with sourcing geography
- [ ] Geographic concentration risks flagged
- [ ] Single-point-of-failure risks identified
- [ ] Customer concentration analyzed
- [ ] 3-5 market entry opportunities outlined

## Pitfalls
- **Assuming supplier list is exhaustive:** Award winners ≠ complete vendor base
- **Overestimating domestic sourcing:** "96% US-sourced" may apply only to specific materials (e.g., steel)
- **Missing hidden dependencies:** Proprietary components often single-source but undisclosed
- **Underestimating customer power:** Retailer concentration (e.g., Lowe's 38% of receivables) creates pricing pressure

## Verification
Output should include: company financials, supplier table with strategic importance, risk analysis with severity ratings, and actionable market entry strategies.
