# Channel Selection Matrix

Not every product belongs on every platform. This matrix helps the skill choose channels based on audience and product characteristics, not convenience.

## Google Search Ads

**Best for:** Solution Aware and Product Aware audiences actively searching.

**When to use:**
- People are searching for solutions to the problem your product solves
- High-intent keywords exist with reasonable CPCs
- The product solves a specific, searchable problem ("invoice template" not "be more productive")
- B2B or professional tools (search intent is high during work hours)

**When NOT to use:**
- The problem is latent (people don't search for it)
- CPCs are >$10 and LTV can't support it
- Category is dominated by big brands bidding aggressively
- Product is impulse/novelty (people don't search for fun tools)

**Typical CPC range:** $1-5 for long-tail, $5-15 for competitive B2B, $10-50+ for finance/legal

**Minimum viable budget:** $15-20/day (need ~10 clicks for signal)

---

## Meta Ads (Facebook + Instagram)

**Best for:** Problem Aware audiences, visual products, impulse purchases.

**When to use:**
- Product solves a problem people don't know they have (interrupt model)
- Visual/demo-friendly product (screenshots, before/after, video)
- Consumer or prosumer audience
- Interest-based targeting makes sense (people who use X would want Y)
- Retargeting website visitors

**When NOT to use:**
- Highly technical B2B (LinkedIn or Google better)
- Product requires extensive education before purchase
- Audience skews 55+ (Facebook still works, but efficiency drops)
- Privacy-sensitive verticals (limited targeting post-iOS 14)

**Typical CPC range:** $0.50-2.00 for broad, $1.00-4.00 for competitive

**Minimum viable budget:** $10-15/day (Meta needs volume to optimize)

---

## LinkedIn Ads

**Best for:** B2B, professional tools, high-LTV products.

**When to use:**
- Product is B2B with LTV > $500
- Targeting by job title, company size, industry is critical
- Professional development, SaaS, business tools
- Content marketing / lead magnet funnels

**When NOT to use:**
- B2C products (too expensive per click)
- Low-LTV products (CPCs of $5-15 require high conversion)
- Impulse purchases

**Typical CPC range:** $5-15, sometimes $20+ for competitive B2B

**Minimum viable budget:** $30-50/day (expensive per click, need volume)

---

## Email Marketing

**Best for:** Nurturing Problem Aware → Product Aware, retaining customers.

**When to use:**
- Funnel includes a lead magnet or free trial
- Product benefits from education before purchase
- Building long-term relationship (SaaS, subscriptions, courses)
- Retargeting site visitors who didn't convert

**When NOT to use:**
- No existing list and no lead magnet (nothing to nurture with)
- Pure impulse purchase products (email slows them down)
- Product doesn't benefit from education

**Cost:** Near-zero per send (Mailchimp free tier: 500 contacts, Resend: 3000/month free)

---

## Content / SEO

**Best for:** Long-term, compounding organic traffic. Solution Aware audiences.

**When to use:**
- Problem is searchable and evergreen
- Willing to invest 3-6 months before seeing results
- Product benefits from education and trust-building
- Competitors' content is weak (opportunity to dominate a niche)

**When NOT to use:**
- Need results this month (SEO is slow)
- Problem is too niche for search volume
- No capacity to produce consistent content

**Cost:** Time investment, minimal cash. Compounds over time.

---

## Decision Framework

**Start with ONE channel.** Don't spread $20/day across 4 platforms — you won't get signal on any of them.

**First channel selection logic:**

1. **Is the problem searchable?** → Start with Google Search
2. **Is the product visual/demo-friendly?** → Start with Meta
3. **Is it B2B with high LTV?** → Start with Google or LinkedIn
4. **Is it a new category nobody searches for?** → Start with Meta (interrupt)
5. **Do you have an existing audience/list?** → Start with Email

**Second channel (after first is profitable or killed):**
- If Google is working → add Meta for retargeting
- If Meta is working → add Google for capture
- If neither works → the product/offer needs work, not more channels

**Budget split when running 2 channels:**
- 60/40 toward the better-performing channel
- Re-evaluate weekly based on blended CPA
- Never split evenly — that's avoiding the decision

## Audience → Channel Mapping Quick Reference

| Audience Type | Primary Channel | Secondary Channel |
|---|---|---|
| "I need to fix X now" (search intent) | Google Search | Meta retargeting |
| "I didn't know this was a problem" (latent) | Meta | Email nurture |
| "I'm evaluating tools for my team" (B2B) | Google Search | LinkedIn |
| "I follow this creator/topic" (community) | Meta / YouTube | Email |
| "I already know you" (warm) | Email | Meta retargeting |
